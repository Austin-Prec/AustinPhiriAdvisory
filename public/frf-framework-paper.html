The Forensic Readiness Framework

A practitioner's framework for proactive forensic infrastructure in institutional environments

The Forensic Readiness Framework (FRF) is a governance methodology for building and maintaining the documentation, verification, and review infrastructure that enables an institution to investigate any financial transaction at any time — with speed, completeness, and evidentiary integrity — regardless of whether fraud has been detected.

Developed by Austin Precious Phiri through twelve years of applied practice in forensic finance, institutional governance, and international development across Southern Africa, the FRF addresses a critical gap in institutional design: most organisations build controls to prevent fraud but neglect the infrastructure needed to investigate it when it occurs.

The FRF comprises five pillars:

Pillar I — Beneficiary Verification Architecture
Structural controls for verifying the existence, eligibility, and entitlement of every beneficiary before and after payment. This pillar ensures that every disbursement can be independently verified against documented evidence of the beneficiary's identity and entitlement.
Pillar II — Payroll Integrity Controls
Verification infrastructure ensuring that every payroll entry corresponds to a verified, active, and authorised individual. This pillar addresses the most common vector for institutional fraud by embedding structural controls in payroll processes.
Pillar III — Vendor and Payee Verification Register
A maintained register of verified vendors and payees with ongoing due diligence and periodic re-verification. This pillar ensures that every payment recipient is known, verified, and subject to ongoing scrutiny.
Pillar IV — Quarterly Forensic Review Protocol
A structured quarterly review process that examines transaction patterns, control adherence, and anomaly indicators. This pillar ensures that forensic readiness is not a one-time exercise but an ongoing institutional discipline.
Pillar V — Board-Led Forensic Governance
Board-level governance structures that ensure forensic readiness is maintained, resourced, and enforced. This pillar places forensic infrastructure under board oversight, ensuring that it receives the institutional priority and resources it requires.

Application

The FRF is designed for application in institutional environments where forensic infrastructure is absent, inadequate, or has been compromised. It is particularly suited to NGOs, professional associations, and development sector organisations operating in Southern Africa that manage multi-donor portfolios and require robust forensic controls.
